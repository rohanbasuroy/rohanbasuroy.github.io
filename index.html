Rohan Basu Roy

I am an incoming Assistant Professor (tenure-track) in the Kahlert School of Computing and SCI Institute at the University of Utah. I perform research on scheduling and optimizations on various forms of parallel and distributed computing systems like cloud computing, serverless platforms, and HPC/supercomputing systems. The goal of my research is to improve the productivity, cost-effectiveness, and sustainability of these large-scale systems. The tools and solutions from my works are largely open-sourced, regularly reproduced and used in the systems research community. 

My works have appeared in several highly competitive systems research venues, in recognition of which I have received the ACM-IEEE CS George Michael Memorial HPC Fellowship, MLCommons ML and Systems Rising Star recognition, and other departmental awards and recognitions. My Ph.D. is in Computer Engineering from Northeastern University Boston, advised by Prof. Devesh Tiwari. 

Hiring multiple PhD students/ Post-Docs for Fall 2025: Love building computing systems and/or passionate about improving environmental sustainability and equity via computing? Interested in scaling systems performance using modern forms of accelerators, quantum computing, or other special-purpose architectures? Join me to perform high-throughput systems research and make a positive impact. The best way to get a response is to provide specific reasons of why you would want to work with me.

Awards and Recognition

2023 ACM/IEEE-CS George Michael Memorial HPC Fellowship

The ACM-IEEE CS George Michael Memorial HPC Fellowship is endowed in memory of George Michael, one of the founders of the SC Conference series. The fellowship honors exceptional PhD students throughout the world whose research focus is on high performance computing applications, networking, storage, or large-scale data analytics using the most powerful computers that are currently available.

Awarded “for methods and tools that exploit cloud computing and on-premise HPC resources to enhance the productivity of computational scientists and the sustainability of HPC.”

Media: ACM Press Release, IEEE Press Release, HPCwire Press Release, Northeastern Global News

2023 MLCommons ML and Systems Rising Star

MLCommons Rising Stars is a cohort of 35 early-to-late-stage and recently graduated PhD students working at the intersection of Machine Learning (ML) and Systems research. The MLCommons Rising Stars program provides a unique platform for a diverse range of emerging researchers to present their research to industry and academic experts, build new skills, and establish academic and industry collaborations. 

Media: MLCommons Press Release

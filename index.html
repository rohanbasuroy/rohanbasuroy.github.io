Rohan Basu Roy

I am an Assistant Professor in the Kahlert School of Computing and the SCI Institute at the University of Utah. I direct the SysHPC Lab, where we build systems and softwares for large-scale and emerging computing platforms. 

My research is on scheduling and resource optimizations of various forms of parallel and distributed systems like cloud computing, serverless platforms, and HPC/supercomputing systems to improve their productivity, cost-effectiveness, and sustainability. Currently, I am also looking into systems and compiler-level optimizations for emerging quantum computing platforms. The tools and artifacts from my works are largely open-sourced, regularly reproduced and used in the systems research community. 

My works have appeared in several highly competitive systems research venues, in recognition of which I have received the IEEE CS Computing's Top 30 Early Career Professionals Award, ACM-IEEE CS George Michael Memorial HPC Fellowship, MLCommons ML and Systems Rising Star recognition, and other departmental awards and recognitions. My Ph.D. was in Computer Engineering from Northeastern University, Boston, advised by Prof. Devesh Tiwari. 

I am looking for Ph.D. students, with interest/experience in HPC and Quantum Computation. If you are motivated, drop me an email and apply to the University of Utah's CS Ph.D. program.
